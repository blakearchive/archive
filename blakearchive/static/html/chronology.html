1757 Born in London (28 November) to James Blake, a hosier, and his wife Catherine Wright Armitage Blake. Lives with family at 28 Broad Street.
1767 Enrolled in Henry Pars's drawing school.
1772 Apprenticed to James Basire, engraver to the Society of Antiquaries and the Royal Society. Begins to live with Basire at 31 Great Queen Street, London.
1779 Apprenticeship ends.
Becomes journeyman copy engraver.
Admitted as student to Royal Academy of Art's Schools of Design.
1780 Gordon Riots in London (June), in which Blake may have participated.
Arrested on suspicion of spying during sketching trip on the River Medway.
1782 Marries Catherine Boucher (18 August). Moves to 23 Green Street, London.
1783 Publishes Poetical Sketches.
1784 Moves to 27 Broad Street, London. Sets up printing and publishing partnership with James Parker at 27 Broad Street.
Begins writing An Island in the Moon (c. 1784-85).
1785 Moves to 28 Poland Street, London.
1787 Younger brother Robert dies (February).
c. 1788 Invents relief etching.
Publishes All Religions are One and There is No Natural Religion, first illuminated books.
1789 Publishes Songs of Innocence and begins The Book of Thel (1789-90).
Writes Tiriel (c. 1789).
Attends organizational meeting of the New Jerusalem (Swedenborgian) Church (April).
1790 Prints The Marriage of Heaven and Hell.
Moves to 13 Hercules Buildings, Lambeth.
1791 Writes at least one part of several proposed parts of The French Revolution.
Composes and engraves six original designs for Mary Wollstonecraft's Original Stories from Real Life.
Begins to engrave seventeen illustrations for John Gabriel Stedman’s Narrative, of a Five Years’ Expedition, Against the Revolted Negroes of Surinam, published in 1796.
1793 Publishes Visions of the Daughters of Albion, America a Prophecy, and For Children: The Gates of Paradise.
Engraves Albion rose.
Prints the Prospectus advertising engravings and illuminated books.
1794 Publishes Europe: a Prophecy, Songs of Experience, and The [First] Book of Urizen.
1795 Publishes The Book of Ahania, The Book of Los, and The Song of Los.
Produces twelve large color-printed drawings.
Begins work on 537 water color illustrations to Edward Young's Night Thoughts.
Engraves Albion rose.
c. 1796 Begins writing Vala, or The Four Zoas.
Produces the Small Book of Designs and the Large Book of Designs.
1797 Begins work on 116 water color illustrations to the poems of Thomas Gray for John Flaxman.
Young's Night Thoughts published with 43 plates engraved by Blake after his own designs.
c. 1799 Begins fifty biblical temperas for Thomas Butts.
1800 Moves to Felpham, Sussex, to work for patron William Hayley.
Begins biblical water colors for Thomas Butts.
1801 Produces eight water color illustrations of Milton's Comus for the Rev. Joseph Thomas.
1803 Indicted on charges of sedition (October) as a result of a quarrel with a soldier, John Scolfield, whom he had evicted from his garden (August).
Returns to London and moves to 17 South Molton Street.
1804 Acquitted of sedition charges (January).
Begins work on Milton a Poem and Jerusalem The Emanation of the Giant Albion.
1805 Begins illustrations for Blair's The Grave, to be published by Robert Cromek.
Produces nineteen water color illustrations of the Book of Job for Thomas Butts (c. 1805-6).
1807 Produces twelve water color illustrations of Milton's Paradise Lost for the Rev. Joseph Thomas.
c. 1807 Produces the Pickering Manuscript.
1808 Produces twelve water color illustrations of Milton's Paradise Lost for Thomas Butts.
c. 1808 Annotates the first eight of Reynolds Discourses.
1809 Exhibits sixteen paintings at 28 Broad Street, accompanied by a Descriptive Catalogue defending his theory and practice.
Produces six water color illustrations of Milton’s On the Morning of Christ’s Nativity for the Rev. Joseph Thomas.
c. 1810-11 Drafts the Public Address.
c. 1811 Prints first copies of Milton a Poem.
1815 Produces eight water color illustrations of Milton's Comus for Thomas Butts.
Produces six water color illustrations of Milton’s On the Morning of Christ’s Nativity for Thomas Butts.
c. 1816-1820 Produces twelve water color illustrations of Milton’s L’Allegro and Il Penseroso for Thomas Butts.
Produces twelve water color illustrations of Milton’s Paradise Regained, possibly for Thomas Butts but sold to John Linnell.
1818 Meets John Linnell.
Begins sketching "Visionary Heads" for John Varley.
c. 1820 Publishes For the Sexes: The Gates of Paradise.
Prints first copies of Jerusalem The Emanation of the Giant Albion.
1821 Virgil's Pastorals, edited by Robert John Thornton, published with wood engravings by Blake.
Produces twenty-one water color illustrations of the Book of Job for John Linnell.
Moves to 3 Fountain Court, London.
1822 Publishes The Ghost of Abel and On Homers Poetry [and] On Virgil (c. 1822).
Produces three water color illustrations of Milton's Paradise Lost for John Linnell.
1824 Begins illustrations to Dante's Divine Comedy and Bunyan's Pilgrim's Progress.
1826 Publishes a title page and twenty-one engraved Illustrations of the Book of Job.
Produces Laocoön (c. 1826).
Begins Genesis Manuscript.
Begins to engrave seven illustrations from the “Inferno” of his Divine Comedy designs.
1827 Dies 12 August in his rooms at 3 Fountain Court.
